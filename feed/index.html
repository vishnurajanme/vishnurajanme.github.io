For people who doesn’t know the Google Pixel 3, it is one among the very few android phones in the market made under the supervision of Google themselves. So it is something like the Apple iPhone of Android phones. Yes, it cost somewhat like the iPhone too, but its worthy enough with all those cool camera tech baked in. Find more in this link

For getting motivation to read the large answer below, a camera comparison of a 10k phone after mod and before mod is given below. Click on the image to download a full resolution version of the same

Images on left are captured with the modded software and on the right using the default camera application.

All pics are unedited, was shot at the same spot in same lighting conditions just a few seconds apart. We can clearly see that the shots from the modded camera software clearly outperforms the stock camera app.

Hope it is clear now. Google Pixel does most of that night sight magic using the software as compared with high end hardware that the OP6, Samsung Galaxy S10, iPhone X and likes do.

Now here comes the question. How to get it in your phone?

Before telling the answer, we have to check whether your phone supports google’s camera2api and a ported Gcam version for your phone is already available.

Click this link to check whether your device is in the list. If it is, glad news. Continue reading the article.

If it is, glad news. Continue reading the article. If it does not, dont worry. Just google Gcam <space> <your phone name> it. Maybe its already there, but not listed as of now.

Next to the phone name in the link above, there will be a link to the Google Camera Port. Just download and install it and you are done. Many phones like the Xiaomi Redmi note 7 pro supports this mode of installation, but for some others, you may have to enable the google camera2api framework before continuing. Else it wont work

So how to enable it?

  1. Install ADB, FASTBOOT drivers and software in your laptop
  2. Connect your phone to the laptop using a cable
  3. Run some commands using the windows powershell
  4. Install the Gcam ports by going to the link I previously listed and Enjoy

Please note that these collaborative steps have been created by XDA devs, members and Technobuzz editors. All credits goes to them.

Also note that all these mods you try have risks associated with it. Especially when tinkering with your phone in fastboot mode (Step 3). If your phone turns out to be a softbrick, or hardbrick, it is you who turned your phone into the so called brick.

Happy Modding.
